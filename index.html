The Process of Making a Pinehole Camera

Nine Steps
Step 1
Tools

I used the following materials: scissors, black tape, shoe boxes, compasses, tin foil, cans, expired ink, brushes, rulers, and frosted flakes.

Step 2
Blacking

I used ink to black out the inside and top of the shoe box. This step was repeated three times, because the ink was thin, it would take many times to get very black.It would be better if I had paint.

Step 3
Gline

This step is to determine the pinhole position more precisely.

Step 4
Abrazining

I cut a little piece out of the can, then I used a frosted piece to make it thin in the middle to get the rounded holes. After that I used a compass to poke a hole about 0.7 mm in diameter.

Step 5
Blacking

I wrapped all the sides of the hole in the middle with black tape to prevent the light from reflecting in the corner.

Step 6
Punching

I cut a hole in the side of the shoe box according to the shape of the side of my phone, so it was easy to put the phone in without opening the top of the shoe box.

Step 7
Relax

This step is to demonstrate how the phone fits into the shoe box.

Step 8
Relax

After putting the tape on, I used my phone's functions of voice-triggered camera and timed camera.That is, when I seal the tape, All I have to say is, "Chesse," and that triggers the photo shoot ten seconds later.

Step 9
Relax

Since the first test was at night, the object was illuminated by multiple lamps. Now all the preparations are done, I could start taking photos.

Problems

  • The first time I see the image.
  • It's clear but dark.
  • After adding the light source.
  • Adjust the Angle of the light source

The Poor Light

My area has been rainy or cloudy lately and the light is not ideal.After several unsatisfactory daytime shooting, I chose to light the object indoors.

Limited Angle

Because I didn't have a tripod or anything like that, I could only photograph the objects directly in front of me.Some of the objects looked better when shot from the side, but my hands weren't stable enough to get the best shots

Most Are Close Shots

Because there wasn't enough light, most of the objects I photographed had to be shot up close.This will ensure that there is enough light in the picture to make the picture sharper

The Frame is Relatively Small

I didn't calculate the distance between the phone and the shoebox, and my phone couldn't capture the whole picture, so there would be some black areas around the picture.

Pictures Taken With Pinholes of Different Sizes

I calculate the best pinhole diameter is about 0.074 mm. So I decided make a pinhole which is around 0.07mm diameter.
